Big Five Personality Test


Big Five Personality Test

The Big Five Personality Test is a widely accepted personality test in the scientific community. It differs from the Myer-Briggs model by providing a trait model of personality, rather than a type model. The results of the test are given in a spectrum along the five traits, openness, conscientiousness, extraversion, agreeableness, and neuroticism. My results say that I am not very open-minded, meaning I am not very creative and tend to stick with more practical and concrete ideas (The Big Five Personality Test, 2020). My conscientiousness rating of 37.5% means that I am rarely goal orientated, and often act on impulse. My low extraversion rating represents how I rarely seek attention, and prefer to stick to myself. Agreeableness represents how often I am to prioritize the need of others over my own, and my rating of 42% means that I often think of myself first, before realising the need of others (The Big Five Personality Test, 2020). Neuroticism is the rating of a person’s tendency to respond to stress with negative emotions. My neuroticism rating is exactly neutral, meaning I can deal with stressful situations decently well. I think these results represent me relatively well, and are in line with the results from the Myers-Briggs test. This test has made me realise that I am rather selfish, and should prioritize others more when working in a team environment. The results from this test also solidifies my need to be more outgoing as that could make communication more fluent with team members. When forming a team, I would prefer to have someone in the team that is high in openness, as that would mean they are very creative in their way of thinking, and would compliment my style well.
